My Doctor, My Friend .....

is an interactive app designed to help foster strong relationships between young patients and their doctors. Confronting my own nervousness of going to the doctor, I questioned WHY I felt that way. Maybe.. if I felt more like I was visiting a friend, than a stranger, I would be more comfortable. Many adults suffer white coat syndrome usually associated with anxieties felt as a young child while going to doctor visits. To help lessen these anxieties, here at My Doctor, My Friend we believe in bridging the gap between patient and doctor at an early age to prevent lifelong fears. Through our app patients can message with their doctor, get rewards for healthy choices, and submit pictures and images to their doctor. By engaging with patients through technology, doctors can help to lessen the fears and anxieties that can occur in young patients and create a more relaxed, calming experience when visiting the doctor.

Research for My Doctor, My Friend .....

Since close to 20% of the population has “white coat syndrome”, (which entails a person’s blood pressure to spike when walking into the doctor’s office), we felt this was a great concern in the community to focus on. It is critical for everyone’s health to receive preventative care, but this is hindered greatly when a person has great anxiety about going to the doctor and neglects getting the preventative care they need. The earlier we start to eliminate these concerns in young children, the more it will help relieve these phobias later as adults. This app is being designed to help parents prep children on what will happen at the doctor’s office. It would also help to establish a relationship with a young child and their pediatrician. Most importantly, it will help a child to grow into adulthood with a healthy view of going to a doctor and receiving the proper medical help, through regular examinations and preventative health screenings they willl need throughpiut their life. We started our research by interviewing many children (below you will see a sample), and a nurse at Cincinnati Children's Hospital, which gave great incite into the needs of children to connect with their medical providers. Surprisingly, none of the children could even remember the name of their physician. Our app would like to solve that problem, and improve the relationship between young patient and doctor.After researching the needs and abilities of children we have decided our target audience will be between 5-10 year olds. Even though the app will be geared towards children, doctors and nurses would use the app to communicate with these young scared patients, hoping to relieve the stress of going to the doctor.
